## Autonomous, adaptive patch management for Red Hat Enterprise Linux: AI agents with Red Hat Satellite

**Session Code:** LT1316

**Type:** Lightning talk

**Topics:** predictive AI, agentic AI

### Schedule

- 10:30 AM - 10:50 AM (20 min), Room: Expo Hall - Discovery Theater 1

### Abstract

Red Hat Satellite is the backbone of Red Hat Enterprise Linux lifecycle and content management, providing the necessary centralized platform for patching. However, the operational process—including vulnerability, prioritization, change risk assessment, environment-specific testing, and dynamic maintenance window scheduling—remains complex, manual, and prone to error.

 

An AI agent, built using large language models (LLMs) and predictive analytics, can be integrated to transform this workflow into an autonomous operation. This agent ingests data from Satellite and Red Hat Lightspeed (formerly known as Red Hat Insights), performs predictive risk assessment of new patches, intelligent prioritization based on business impact, self-optimizing scheduling to minimize downtime, and autonomous failure remediation by analyzing logs and generating corrective actions.

 

This shift moves patch management from a reactive, scheduled task to a proactive, intent-driven, and continuously optimized system, substantially enhancing operational efficiency, security posture, and system reliability in dynamic hybrid cloud environments.

 

Key takeaways from this session will include:

	Understanding the key bottlenecks in traditional Red Hat Satellite patching workflows.
	Learning how an autonomous AI layer can perform predictive risk assessment and intelligent prioritization of errata.
	Seeing how this agent enhances operational efficiency by self-optimizing scheduling and executing autonomous actions.

## Fine-grained scaling for LLM inference on Red Hat OpenShift: The promise of DRA

**Session Code:** LT2676

**Type:** Lightning talk

**Topics:** AI infrastructure and frameworks, generative AI, AI inference, open source

### Schedule

- 04:20 PM - 04:40 PM (20 min), Room: Expo Hall - Discovery Theater 1

### Abstract

As generative AI adoption surges, organizations face a critical challenge: how to serve large language models (LLMs) efficiently without overprovisioning costly graphics processing unit (GPU) resources.

llm-d is a Kubernetes-native distributed inference platform for scaling LLM serving by intelligently routing requests and disaggregating the inference process into prefill and decode stages across multiple vLLM instances.

The real challenge is scaling LLM inference without wasting GPU resources. It’s not just about NVIDIA Multi-Instance GPU (MIG) slices or time-slicing anymore—we need smarter, fine-grained sharing techniques that minimize idle capacity and maximize efficiency. This means dynamically allocating GPU memory and compute across multiple models, while ensuring cooperation with the Kubernetes scheduler so resource optimization never violates Pod requirements. The goal is smaller, smarter scaling strategies that go beyond static partitioning for cost-effective, high-performance inference.

In this lightning talk, we explore the Kubernetes community’s dynamic resource allocation (DRA) feature—why it’s a promising foundation for fine-grained GPU resource management and what extensions are still needed to achieve efficient LLM inference at scale. We’ll highlight capabilities such as PrioritizedList, PartitionableDevices, and the recently introduced ConsumableCapacity, and explain how they enable dynamic resource requests and adjustments. Through real-world examples, we’ll demonstrate how DRA can help serve multiple models of varying sizes efficiently and cost-effectively on Red Hat OpenShift.

### Speakers

- **Sunyanan Choochotkaew**, Staff Research Scientist
