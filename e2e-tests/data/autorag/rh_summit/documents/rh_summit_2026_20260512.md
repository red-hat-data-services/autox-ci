## GPUs take flight: Safety-first multi-tenant platform engineering with NVIDIA and Red Hat OpenShift AI

**Session Code:** LT1116

**Type:** Lightning talk

**Topics:** AI infrastructure and frameworks, DevSecOps, AI inference

### Schedule

- 10:30 AM - 10:50 AM (20 min), Room: Expo Hall - Discovery Theater 1

### Abstract

Running shared, production-grade graphics processing units (GPUs) for avionics demands predictability, isolation, and traceability. In this lightning talk, the presenter will share an experience-driven pattern for multi-tenant GPU platforms on Red Hat OpenShift AI with NVIDIA G/H200—fronted by an ingress layer and tailored for regulated aerospace constraints.

The talk covers practical guardrails, including:

	Driver and firmware pinning with long-life kernels.
	Image provenance with a software bill of materials (SBOM) and attestation.
	Policy with Kyverno and Falco.
	Audit-friendly observability using precision time protocol (PTP) time sync with NVIDIA Data Center GPU Manager (DCGM) and tracing.

A short demo will walk you through a repo with manifests, checks, and a staged rollout workflow. Attendees will see how to:

	Structure tenant isolation (namespaces, quotas, priority/preemption).
	Choose NVIDIA Multi-Instance GPU (MIG) versus full-GPU for predictable performance.
	Schedule with topology awareness across heterogeneous nodes.
	Build low-jitter inference paths (Non-uniform memory access (NUMA) or peripheral component interconnect express (PCIe) locality, pinned central processing unit (CPU), network considerations)

You'll leave with a reusable checklist and Day-2 playbooks (upgrade and rollback, autoscaling, cost and chargeback) that can be applied immediately in highly regulated and aerospace-like environments.

Learn this advanced security skill set, including:

	SELinux: Move beyond basic permissive and enforcing modes to architect and troubleshoot custom Mandatory Access Control (MAC) policies, significantly limiting the damage from exploited processes. This will help you detect compromised services and privilege escalation.
	Keylime: Remote attestation and integrity monitoring protects against unauthorized modifications to the bootloader, kernel, or critical system files. In the last year, he has contributed to high-impact projects like Red Hat OpenShift AI, Red Hat Trusted Application Pipeline, Red Hat OpenShift Virtualization, and Red Hat Ansible Automation Platform.

## Late-night labs: App platform

**Session Code:** LB2897

**Type:** Reception

### Schedule

- 06:00 PM - 08:30 PM (150 min), Room: A314 - Level 3

### Abstract

Trade the traditional networking mixer for a terminal window and a cold drink. Late-night labs is a one-night-only event offering an extensive selection of our most popular labs for self-paced exploration in a relaxed, low-pressure environment.

Whether you want to catch a lab you missed during the day or just prefer a quiet place to build, we’ve got you covered. What to expect:

	Broad access: Choose from a range of our hands-on labs to tackle at your own pace.
	No-pressure networking: Red Hat product managers and engineers are available to talk shop and answer your questions in detail.
	Fuel and refreshments: Skip the small talk and head straight for the food and drinks—we’ll have them available throughout the night.
	Prizes: Successfully finish a lab and you’ll be entered into our drawing at the end of the night for a chance to win.

If you prefer hands-on-keyboard learning over traditional networking events, come grab a seat and join us for a low-key, productive evening.
